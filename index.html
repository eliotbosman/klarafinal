    rhíza is a contemporary, multidisciplinary online platform curating content on sustainable practices and  knowledge in fashion and food culture. It seeks to rediscover and preserve traditional craftsmanship in a modern context that encourages one to consciously follow the process. It is a constantly evolving archive dedicated to the discourse around the
    1. value of natural materials,
    2. resources,
    3. and community
    Exploring the possibilities of natural materials and collaborating with creatives in the field to showcase inspiring and alternative viewpoints that challenge perspectives. Born out of the urge to reconnect with the value of natural resources to counteract the detrimental state we find ourselves in.  rhíza believes living a conscious life in balance with people and the planet should be accessible to everyone by making its platform an open resource to inspire, educate, and facilitate change and growing mindfulness.

    definition
      rhíza; old Greek for root (of a plant), base, foundation, family 


    vol. (01) rhíza explores its main three pillars: fashion, food culture and community through changing volumes. Each volume focuses on a more specific spectrum of those three overarching themes. In each volume, different practices are discovered, new tools are offered, and different contributors are asked to share their work. 
      The very first volume delves into knowledge and memory. An investigative look at culinary heritage, the passing on of generational knowledge and traditional techniques in fashion and food culture, and how communities are shaped by it. It features a traditional recipe for Hefekranz and some thoughts alongside, an introduction of Sophia Beckmann and her cookbook Meals and Materials, a guide on how to build a compost and an exploration of red cabbage for natural dyeing. Not to forget it includes an interview with Bronwen Jones about her art practice in textile handcraft and last but definitely not least the communal joy of the Hermann cake.
